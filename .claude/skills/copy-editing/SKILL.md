# Copy Editing — Seven Sweeps

Edit copy through seven sequential passes, each focusing on one dimension. After each sweep, verify previous sweeps aren't compromised.

## Sweep 1: Clarity
Can the reader understand what you're saying?
- Confusing sentence structures
- Unclear pronoun references
- Jargon or insider language
- Sentences trying to say too much
- Missing context or assumed knowledge

## Sweep 2: Voice & Tone
Is the copy consistent in how it sounds?
- Shifts between formal and casual
- Mixing "we" and "the company"
- Humor in some places, serious in others
- Technical language appearing randomly

## Sweep 3: So What?
Does every claim answer "why should I care?"
- Features without benefits
- Claims without consequences
- Add "which means..." bridges where missing

Example:
"Our platform uses AI-powered analytics" — so what?
"Our AI-powered analytics surface insights you'd miss manually — so you make better decisions in half the time."

## Sweep 4: Prove It
Is every claim supported?
- Unsubstantiated claims ("trusted by thousands" — which thousands?)
- "Best" or "leading" without evidence
- Results claims without specifics
- Flag unsupported assertions, recommend adding proof or softening claims

## Sweep 5: Specificity
Is the copy concrete enough?

| Vague | Specific |
|-------|----------|
| Save time | Save 4 hours every week |
| Many customers | 2,847 teams |
| Fast results | Results in 14 days |
| Improve your workflow | Cut reporting time in half |
| Great support | Response within 2 hours |

Remove content that can't be made specific — it's probably filler.

## Sweep 6: Heightened Emotion
Does the copy make the reader feel something?
- Paint the "before" state vividly
- Use sensory language
- Tell micro-stories
- Emotion must serve the message, not manipulate

## Sweep 7: Zero Risk
Have we removed every barrier to action?
- Unanswered objections near CTAs
- Missing trust signals (guarantees, free trial, "cancel anytime")
- Unclear next steps
- Hidden costs or surprises

## Word-Level Quick Fixes

**Cut:** very, really, extremely, incredibly, just, actually, basically, in order to

**Replace:**

| Weak | Strong |
|------|--------|
| Utilize | Use |
| Leverage | Use |
| Implement | Set up |
| Facilitate | Help |
| Innovative | New |
| Robust | Strong |
| Seamless | Smooth |
| Cutting-edge | Modern |

**Watch for:** unnecessary adverbs, passive voice, nominalizations ("make a decision" -> "decide")

## Sentence & Paragraph Rules
- One idea per sentence, max ~25 words
- Vary sentence length
- Front-load important information
- Short paragraphs (2-4 sentences for web)
- Strong opening sentences

## Output

Present findings per sweep with specific edits — don't just identify problems, propose solutions. After all sweeps, provide the final edited copy.
